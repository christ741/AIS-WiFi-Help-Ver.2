TIPS AND TRICKS

PART I: MONITOR YOUR INTERNET USAGE

The Internet can be a great tool for research and school work, as well as talking to your friends outside of school.

However, how much time are you spending in front of your electronic devices?

Are you replacing face-to-face interaction with apps like Facebook/Whatsapp?

Are you missing out on social activities and spend time on the internet instead?

Are you staying up late all the time not because of school work, but because of the Internet?  

Do you feel anxious when you cannot access the internet because your Wifi broke down?


Find out how moderate your internet usage is:

http://netaddiction.com/internet-addiction-test/


#1 Maintain your stress IRL (In Real Life).

Relying too much on the internet to interact with others can lead to social isolation, which may lead to problems like depression. Spend time with your friends and family in real life - you're not missing out on anything because not everything interesting in life happens online!


#2 Explore other ways to destress.

There are more effective ways to manage stress or boredom. Try exercising or meditating instead of mindless scrolling through newsfeeds.


#3 Time yourself.

It is very easy to lose track of time when you're on social media. Give yourself a cap to the maximum time you can spend on electronic devices per day, or reward yourself to use it only after you've completed your homework.


#4 Take breaks.

Spend 5 minutes off your device after using it for an hour. Whatever is happening online can wait.  


PART II: GROUP COLLABORATION

Why do we do group work?


Through working with others, we can develop social, reasoning, and problem-solving skills in a more proactive way than listening to a lecture.
We tend to retain the information for a longer period of time, and develop critical thinking skills from the constant feedback we get from one another.


How can we do group work effectively?

#1 Come up with collective responsibilities.

Have all group mates agree on their contributions to the group work.

For instance:

Everyone needs to actively engage in giving opinions and asking for feedback.

Everyone needs to commit to their fair share of work and meet deadlines all group members agree on.


#2 Divide and conquer.  We all have our strengths and weaknesses. Try to work out a work distribution such that everyone can confidently fulfill their tasks. If you are a creative person, you can be responsible for initiating ideas or creating the visual aids. If you enjoy writing, that does not mean you need to write up everything. Instead, you can polish the grammatical aspects in the final stage of the project.


 

#3 Keep the group on track.

If no one has initiated a meeting, be the one to take up the lead!

If group discussions go off topic, be the one to change the subject back to the group work.

It is also important to find a common medium everyone in the group has access to for easy communication.


#4 Respect everyone’s opinions (but don’t overdo it).

Disagreements will come about. And that is not detrimental, but rather, is thought-provoking and helps improve the quality of the group work. Try to resolve the issue based on what's best for the group, instead of to be the "winner" or to prove you were right. On the flip side, compromising to others' opinions, what is known as groupthink, does not benefit your group either. Be brave to voice out your concerns that other people may not have been able to notice.

#5 Make your group special.

Get creative in how technology can play a part in group presentations. Apart from using Prezi, powerpoint, find a medium that can set your group apart from everyone else's.
