PART I: MONITOR YOUR INTERNET USAGE

 

The Internet can be a great tool for research and school work, as well as talking to your friends outside of school.

However, how much time are you spending in front of your electronic devices?

Are you replacing face-to-face interaction with apps like Facebook/Whatsapp?

Are you missing out on social activities and spend time on the internet instead?

Are you staying up late all the time not because of school work, but because of the Internet?  

Do you feel anxious when you cannot access the internet because your Wifi broke down?

Find out how moderate your internet usage is here.

#1 Maintain your stress IRL (In Real Life).

 

Relying too much on the internet to interact with others can lead to social isolation, which may lead to problems like depression. Spend time with your friends and family in real life - you're not missing out on anything because not everything interesting in life happens online!


 

#2 Explore other ways to destress.

 

There are more effective ways to manage stress or boredom. Try exercising or meditating instead of mindless scrolling through newsfeeds.
